# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of Webox pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, safe, and constructive experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We want Webox to be a place where people can:

- ask good-faith questions,
- disagree about architecture without attacking people,
- learn in public,
- review code rigorously without contempt,
- contribute documentation, research, design, tests, or code without being treated as second-class contributors.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment:

- Showing empathy and patience.
- Giving actionable, technical feedback instead of contemptuous commentary.
- Assuming good faith until there is evidence otherwise.
- Being explicit about uncertainty, trade-offs, and risk.
- Respecting different levels of experience.
- Accepting correction gracefully.
- Focusing criticism on code, design, decisions, and process rather than on people.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or stalking.
- Insults, slurs, ad hominem attacks, or degrading language.
- Doxxing or exposing private information without consent.
- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, baiting, dogpiling, or deliberately derailing technical discussion.
- Publishing someone else's private correspondence without permission.
- Repeatedly ignoring project boundaries after moderation.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- setting and enforcing the tone of discussion,
- explaining moderation decisions clearly,
- protecting contributors from harassment,
- separating sharp technical review from personal hostility,
- acting consistently and proportionally.

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, discussions, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues,
- pull requests and review threads,
- discussions,
- commit messages,
- documentation review,
- any private communication initiated in the context of project contribution.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project maintainer through a **private channel**.

Until a dedicated public contact address is configured for the repository, enforcement reports should be sent privately to the maintainer rather than posted in public issues or discussions.

All reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as possible. The maintainer will:

- protect the privacy and safety of the reporter as much as possible,
- investigate in good faith,
- keep the response proportional to the behavior,
- prioritize safety over convenience.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Possible responses include:

1. **Correction**  
   A private or public clarification that a specific behavior was inappropriate.

2. **Warning**  
   A formal warning with clear expectations for future behavior.

3. **Temporary restriction**  
   Temporary loss of access to some or all project interaction spaces.

4. **Permanent ban**  
   Removal from project spaces when behavior is severe, repeated, or clearly incompatible with community safety.

## Project-Specific Interpretation

Webox is a technically opinionated project. Strong disagreement is acceptable. Sharp review is acceptable. High standards are acceptable.

What is **not** acceptable:

- humiliating less experienced contributors,
- using expertise as a weapon,
- replacing technical argument with status games,
- treating documentation, tests, or research work as inferior to code,
- normalizing hostility in the name of "directness."
